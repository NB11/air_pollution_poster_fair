Ground-based air quality monitoring networks exhibit sparse and uneven geographic coverage, limiting fine-scale pollutant mapping crucial for public health assessment. Building upon the single-pollutant NO₂ model of Scheibenreif et al. (2022), this project presents a remote sensing deep learning framework for simultaneous multi-pollutant temporal estimation at 10 m spatial resolution. By fusing Sentinel-2 surface reflectance with Sentinel-5P tropospheric column products and ERA5 meteorological data, the model estimates monthly ground-level concentrations of NO₂, O₃, PM₁₀, PM₂.₅, and SO₂. The multi-modal architecture was trained on 8,698 monitoring sites spanning 74 months (2018–2024), enabling intra-urban gradient mapping for exposure assessment applications.
