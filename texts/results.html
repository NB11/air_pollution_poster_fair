The global multi-pollutant model (Model C, excluding SO₂) achieved strong performance for gaseous species with direct satellite retrievals: NO₂ reached R² = 0.57 (MAE = 6.27 μg/m³), exceeding the baseline from Scheibenreif et al. (2022). O₃ demonstrated the highest accuracy with R² = 0.72 (MAE = 11.10 μg/m³), benefiting from photochemical coupling and stable atmospheric signals.

Particulate matter predictions proved more challenging due to the absence of direct Sentinel-5P products. PM₁₀ achieved moderate performance (R² = 0.41, MAE = 11.23 μg/m³) by leveraging optical features and meteorological covariates. PM₂.₅ showed limited correlation (R² = 0.14, MAE = 7.64 μg/m³), likely constrained by the ResNet50 backbone's land-cover pretraining rather than atmospheric aerosol signatures. A dedicated single-target PM₂.₅ model (Model D) improved performance to R² = 0.19.

SO₂ estimation failed across all architectures (R² ≤ 0.01), attributed to sparse point-source emissions (industrial stacks, volcanic plumes) incompatible with the shared-backbone approach.
